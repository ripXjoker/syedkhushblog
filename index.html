Echoes of Existence

Brief inquiries into the fabric of being.

What is dream?

A nocturnal theatre where the self auditions for other selves, scripted by echoes of the day and whispers from the void.

Why do we cry at born?

The first protest against the shock of separation, the raw symphony of air filling lungs once cradled in silence.

What exactly happens after death?

The grandest question mark, perhaps a return to the stardust from whence we came, or the silence before a new song.

Why is everything the way it is?

A tapestry woven from chance and necessity, each thread a consequence, its pattern perceived only in retrospect, if at all.

What are goosebumps?

The skin's memory of ancient chills and primal awe, a fleeting armor against the unseen or the deeply felt.

What is déjà vu?

A hiccup in time's perception, the fleeting echo of a life unlived, or a momentary glimpse of the eternal recurrence.

What is the butterfly effect?

The universe whispering that the smallest flutter of will can sculpt mountains in the landscape of becoming.
